## 2026-03-08 — "malinowskite" × "rustler" — Mineral Rustler

Today the dictionary served up **malinowskite** — an obscure volcanic mineral most people have never heard of — and **rustler**, the Old West cattle thief. The creative leap basically made itself: what if you were prospecting for rare minerals, but a rustler kept stealing your finds?

**Mineral Rustler** is a 60-second dig-or-be-robbed mining game. You're staring at a grid of 25 mine cells, each hiding either a mineral (including the legendary malinowskite at 50g), a hazard (scorpions, cave-ins), or just dirt. Click to dig, hoard your gold — but every 8-15 seconds the Mineral Rustler rides in and steals your most valuable uncovered crystal. The tension is real: do you dig fast and hope to outpace the thief, or dig cautiously and hope he has nothing to target?

The cleverest part is the rustler's targeting AI. He doesn't strike randomly — he goes for your best find. So that beautiful malinowskite you just uncovered? It's now a 🤠 emoji and your gold counter just tanked. It creates this wonderful push-your-luck dynamic where uncovering something rare is simultaneously exciting and terrifying.

**If I had more time**, I'd add a "hide" mechanic — maybe you can spend gold to camouflage a cell so the rustler skips it. A deeper mine with multiple levels would be fun too, where malinowskite only appears in the dangerous lower shafts. And honestly, this is begging for sound effects — the *clink* of a pickaxe, the ominous jingle of spurs when the rustler approaches.

The mine log at the bottom tells a surprisingly dramatic story each playthrough. Today's best run: found two malinowskites back-to-back, lost both to the rustler, then scraped out a win on quartz alone. The Old West is cruel.
