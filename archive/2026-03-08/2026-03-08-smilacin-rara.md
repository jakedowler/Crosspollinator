## 2026-03-08 — "smilacin" × "rara" — Rare Roots: The Botanical Carnival

What do a plant glycoside and a Haitian street carnival have in common? More than you'd think. Smilacin is a compound found in Smilax plants — greenbriers and sarsaparilla vines that thread through tropical forests. Rara is Haiti's explosive carnival tradition of music, dance, and community parades. The connection? Both are rooted in the tropics, both are about hidden abundance, and both reward those who go looking.

**Rare Roots** is a garden-growing game with a gacha twist. You plant seeds in a 5×5 grid, and each plot randomly yields a smilacin-producing plant — from humble sprouts (1mg) all the way up to the mythic Smilacin Star (500mg). The rarity system spans common through mythic, with legendary and mythic discoveries triggering particle bursts and golden borders. The real hook: collect three rare-or-better blooms and you unlock a *Rara Carnival* — a full-screen confetti explosion, a scrolling emoji parade of dancers and drums, and a bonus smilacin payout.

The cleverest bit is how the carnival mechanic creates a compulsion loop. You're not just planting for the joy of collecting — you're chasing the next carnival trigger. Each rare bloom feels like progress toward a party, and the parade payoff (complete with animated confetti physics and a scrolling band of 🎺🥁💃🕺) is genuinely delightful. The rarity counter resets after each carnival, so the cycle begins anew.

**If I had more time**, I'd add seasonal events where certain rare plants only bloom during specific "months," cross-pollination mechanics where adjacent plants influence each other's rarity, and a smilacin marketplace where you trade accumulated compounds for garden upgrades. A sound design pass with actual rara music samples would be the chef's kiss.

Something about watching a garden erupt into spontaneous celebration never gets old.
